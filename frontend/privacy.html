Privacy Policy

How Ferox Times collects, uses, and protects personal information across accounts, subscriptions, and day-to-day site usage.

Effective date: March 29, 2026

At Ferox Times, your privacy and data security are of utmost importance. This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, process, and protect your personal information when you access our digital platform, applications, and newsletters. By interacting with Ferox Times, you agree to the practices described in this document.

This policy is designed to comply with global privacy standards, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA).

1. Information We Collect

  • Personal Identification Information: Name, email address, and profile details provided when creating an account, subscribing to newsletters, or applying as an independent contributor.
  • Device and Usage Data: IP addresses, browser types, operating systems, and interaction metrics on our site. We collect this directly and via automated technology like Google Analytics 4 (GA4).
  • Communication Data: Records of your correspondence with our editorial, advertising, or support teams.

2. How We Use Your Information

We do not sell your personal data. Your information is strictly used to:

  • Deliver breaking news, tailored content, and daily newsletters.
  • Manage your user account, saved articles, and profile preferences.
  • Analyze traffic patterns to optimize platform performance and journalistic reach.
  • Serve personalized or contextual advertisements to fund our independent reporting (e.g., via Google AdSense).
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms of Service.

3. Third-Party Services & Tracking

We integrate selected third-party services to deliver a premium user experience. These include Google Analytics (for performance metrics), Google AdSense (for ad delivery), and social media widgets (such as Facebook and X). These third parties may utilize cookies and web beacons to track your online activities across different websites. For more details, please review our Cookie Policy.

4. Web Push Notifications

If you opt-in to receive browser push notifications for breaking news, a unique device identifier is securely stored. You can revoke this permission at any time directly through your browser or device settings.

5. Your Privacy Rights (GDPR & CCPA)

Depending on your jurisdiction, you hold specific rights regarding your personal data:

  • The Right to Access & Portability: You can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • The Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): You can request the permanent deletion of your account and associated personal data.
  • The Right to Opt-Out: You may opt-out of marketing communications and personalized advertising at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact our Data Protection Office.
